Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a classic Italian pasta made from durum wheat and water, typically served with a variety of sauces such as marinara, Alfredo, or Bolognese.

Known for its long, thin strands, spaghetti is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed with an array of ingredients, making it a staple in many households around the world.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti: An 8-ounce package of spaghetti should make about eight servings.
  • Beef: Youâ€™ll need a pound of lean ground beef. You can use a mix of ground beef and ground turkey, if you prefer.
  • Spaghetti sauce: Use a jar of store-bought spaghetti sauce or make your own marinara sauce at home.
  • Butter: Use a stick of sliced butter to prepare the pan and to add richness and flavor to the baked spaghetti.
  • Cottage cheese, cream cheese, and sour cream: A mixture of cottage cheese, cream cheese, and sour cream is essential for this decadent, creamy spaghetti sauce.
  • Cheese: Shredded Cheddar cheese is the perfect finishing touch for this million dollar spaghetti.

Steps

  1. Boil the spaghetti in salted water, then drain and set aside.
  2. Cook the beef on the stove and discard the grease. Transfer to a bowl and mix in the spaghetti sauce.
  3. Prepare the baking dish, then spread half of the noodles in the bottom.
  4. Top with the mixture of cottage cheese, cream cheese, and sour cream.
  5. Cover with remaining spaghetti and butter.
  6. Pour the beef mixture over the spaghetti and spread in an even layer.
  7. Bake for 30 minutes, top with cheese, and continue baking until the cheese is melted.
